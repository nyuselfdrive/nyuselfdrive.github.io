NYU SelfDrive

About Us

At its core, NYU SelfDrive aims to be at the forefront of designing and prototyping technological advances in the ever-growing field of self-driving cars. Based in NYU Tandon School of Engineering, a global education leader with a world-class faculty and state-of-the-art labs producing groundbreaking research, NYU SelfDrive is dedicated to creating unique ideas in order to develop a product that can revolutionize the automotive industry by implementing computer science algorithms and building a drivable car from scratch.

Our mission is to build the world's safest driver

Research

Our research team consists of students from all years and multiple disciplines. We are working on areas like path planning algorithms, speed estimation using stereo cameras, imitation learning, and behavior cloning.

Build

With a group of students from both mechanical and electrical engineering backgrounds, NYU SelfDrive is able to construct a full-size functioning car with weatherproof precautions to take on the road, along with a mini car for testing.

Compete

NYU SelfDrive attended, for the first time, the annual Intelligent Ground Vehicle Competition and successfully placed in the top 3 for the Self-Drive Challenge, out of the 26 teams in total. In 2019, our team is working in full gear toward the 27th IGVC competition.

IGVC

The IGVC (Intelligent Ground Vehicle Competition) is an annual international robotics competition that our team competes in, which usually takes place at Oakland University in Rochester, Michigan. Undergraduate and graduate students compete to design, power and construct autonomous vehicles that can follow lanes, detect obstacles, and follow waypoint navigation.

Design Competition

The design competition includes a written design report, an oral presentation, and an examination of the vehicle.

Autonomous Challenge

A fully autonomous ground vehicle must navigate an outdoor obstacle course. The course consists of a lane painted with white or yellow lines on a grassy field. Obstacles come in a wide variety of shapes and colors including barrels, cones, saw horses, and posts

Navigation Challenge

This event requires the vehicle to travel autonomously from a specified starting point to a sequence of destinations specified by latitude and longitude. Various obstacles are placed around the course including a fence with a randomly relocated opening.
